Research Interests

My research path started with speech processing and signal representations, then moved into fuzzy set similarity and kernel-based machine learning with a focus on uncertainty-aware modeling.

Through academic and industrial collaboration, this evolved into interpretable machine learning for high-impact domains such as energy analytics, and later into applied AI innovations reflected in patents for weather forecasting, simulation, and operational decision support. More recently, my work has included weather generators, weather downscaling with foundation models, and generative approaches based on diffusion models and GANs for robust environmental AI systems.

My current interests include practical AI, AI foundations, and learning how to deploy AI systems efficiently, focusing on the core principles and technologies that combine scientific rigor with deployable systems.
